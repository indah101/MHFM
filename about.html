About

Maternal Health Initiatives for Mothers is a Nigerian (MHIM) non-governmental organization dedicated to addressing the critical challenges of maternal and child health in the country. Established to reduce maternal and infant mortality, MHIM provides comprehensive healthcare, education, mental health support, economic empowerment, and community outreach programs. The organization also advocates for equitable policies to foster healthy and vibrant communities.
The foundation’s mission is motivated by alarming health statistics, including Nigeria’s maternal mortality ratio of 512 deaths per 100,000 live births (significantly above the global average) and an infant mortality rate of 74 deaths per 1,000 live births.

  • Vision:

    Vision A world where every mother and child is empowered to thrive, fostering healthy and vibrant communities.

  • Mission:

    Enhancing the well-being of mothers and their families through healthcare, education, mental health support, and economic empowerment while advocating for equitable policies.

The foundation’s mission is motivated by alarming health statistics, including Nigeria’s maternal mortality ratio of 512 deaths per 100,000 live births (significantly above the global average) and an infant mortality rate of 74 deaths per 1,000 live births.

Programs and Initiatives

Championing Safe Motherhood and Child Health: Ensuring Dignity, Care, and Hope for Every Family.

Antenatal Care Education Program:

Offers physical and virtual courses and healthcare professional training to improve antenatal care access.

Mental Health Support Program:

Provides counseling and support groups, focusing on postpartum depression.

Women’s Livelihoods Program:

Empowers women through entrepreneurship education, seed capital, and mentorship.

Policy & Advocacy Program:

Conducts research and advocacy campaigns for maternal and child health policies.

Mama Pikin Program:

Provides scholarships and community outreach for underserved communities.

Achievements and Milestones

MHIM’s programs have significantly advanced antenatal care, mental health support, and economic empowerment for women while driving systemic policy changes in maternal health. In 2024, through its Maternal Health Initiatives for Mothers outreach project, the foundation provided targeted training for nurses and midwives, equipping them with enhanced skills and resources to better support expectant mothers. Additionally, the outreach directly engaged expectant mothers through an antenatal crash course, which offered essential education on maternal care. Participants also received birthing kits, ensuring safer delivery experiences and improved maternal outcomes.
